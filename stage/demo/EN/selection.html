Timber Selection

Teak, rosewood and other rare timber species are selected for their advantageous physical properties and renewed vitality.

Rosewood is known for its high density, hardness and heaviness, with prominently dark-red grains, even structure, elegant texture similar to tiger hide, and slightly mellow aroma. It can be also applied as a desirable therapy for blood vessel diastole and relief of stress and fatigue. The rare wood has a long history of use particularly among the scholar-bureaucrats during the Ming and Qing dynasties (1368-1911). Now the material is getting scarcely available and rarer than ever with a high collective value.

The Myanmar teak is a world recognized rare species for its perforated structure, high stability against distortion, wide adaption to changes of humidity, stunning resistance to water penetration, fire or decay, as well as the special teak oil aroma. Moreover, the planed sides of teak may be gradually oxidized into an eye-catching golden color . So apart from its applicability, the teak-made artworks are of great aesthetic values.
